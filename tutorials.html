SISIG

Yield: 4–6 servings

Prep: 25 mins (plus boiling/grilling) | Cook: 25–30 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork maskara (face/ears/jowl) or pork belly (easier substitute)
  • 150 g pork liver (optional but traditional)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3–4 calamansi (or 2 Tbsp lemon/lime)
  • 2–3 Thai chilies (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust to heat)
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp pepper
  • 2 Tbsp oil (plus more as needed)

Steps

  1. Pre-cook the meat: Boil pork (and liver separately) in salted water until tender (pork 45–60 mins; liver 8–10 mins). Drain well.
  2. Char for flavor: Grill or pan-sear pork until edges are slightly crisp and smoky.
  3. Chop fine: Dice pork and liver into small, even bits (the finer, the better texture).
  4. Mix base: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  5. Sauté: Heat oil, sauté onions until translucent. Add chopped pork and liver; toss 2–3 mins.
  6. Season & heat: Pour in the sauce mix. Add chilies. Stir-fry until slightly dry and sizzling.

Pro Tips

  • Texture is king: Boil → grill → chop → quick sauté gives authentic crisp-tender bits.
  • Chicken or tofu sisig: Use chopped grilled chicken thighs or extra-firm tofu, same seasoning.

TOCINO

Yield: 6 servings

Prep: 15 mins • Cure: 24–48 hrs (chilled) • Cook: 10–12 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork kasim (shoulder) or liempo (belly), sliced ¼-inch thick
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1½ tsp fine salt
  • 6 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 Tbsp pineapple juice (tenderizes)
  • 1 Tbsp vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp annatto (achuete) water or powder (color)
  • Optional: ¼ tsp curing salt (Prague Powder #1) if doing longer cures

Steps

  1. Mix cure: Combine sugar, salt, garlic, pineapple juice, vinegar, annatto.
  2. Coat pork: Massage cure into slices evenly.
  3. Cure safely: Place in a covered container. Refrigerate 24–48 hours.
  4. Pan-cook: Put pork in a pan with ¼ cup water. Simmer until water evaporates.
  5. Caramelize: As fat renders, the sugar will caramelize. Add a little oil if pan is dry; cook until glossy and slightly charred at the edges.
  6. Serve: Best with garlic rice and egg (tocilog).

Pro Tips

  • Don’t burn: Keep heat medium; sugar burns fast.
  • Juicier tocino: Use shoulder; belly is richer but fattier.
  • Food safety: Always cure in the fridge (never at room temp).

KARE-KARE

Yield: 6 servings

Prep: 20 mins • Cook: 1½–2 hrs (faster with pressure cooker)

Ingredients

  • 1–1.2 kg oxtail and/or tripe (or pork hock as alternative)
  • 1 onion, quartered; 4 cloves garlic, smashed (for boiling)
  • 1–2 Tbsp annatto (achuete) seeds in ½ cup warm water (or 2 tsp powder)
  • ¾–1 cup ground roasted peanuts or ¾ cup natural peanut butter
  • 2–3 Tbsp toasted ground rice (pang-lapot; for thickening)
  • Vegetables: 1 banana blossom (sliced), 1 large eggplant (chunks), 200 g sitaw (cut), 1 bunch pechay/ bok choy
  • Salt & pepper
  • On the side: bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste)

Steps

  1. Tenderize meat: Boil oxtail (and tripe separately) with onion, garlic, and salt until soft (oxtail 1.5–2 hrs; tripe 2–3 hrs). Skim scum. Reserve broth. (Pressure cooker: 35–45 mins.)
  2. Annatto extract: Soak annatto seeds in warm water; strain to get red liquid (or dissolve powder).
  3. Make peanut base: In a pot, whisk peanut butter/ground peanuts with 3–4 cups reserved broth until smooth.
  4. Thicken: Stir in annatto liquid and toasted ground rice. Simmer gently until glossy and thick.
  5. Add meat: Add tender meat to the sauce; simmer 10 mins to absorb flavor.
  6. Blanch veg: Separately blanch banana blossom, eggplant, sitaw, and pechay in salted water until just tender.
  7. Assemble & serve: Arrange vegetables around the stew or fold in gently. Serve hot with bagoong on the side (add to taste at the table).

Pro Tips

  • Don’t over-salt the stew: Kare-kare is intentionally mild; the bagoong provides the salty-umami kick.
  • Silky sauce: Toast rice in a dry pan then grind; it adds body without graininess.
  • Cleaner broth: Chill broth and remove solidified fat before making the sauce.

BRINGHE

Yield: 6–8 servings

Prep: 20 mins • Cook: 35–45 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice (malagkit), rinsed
  • 1 cup regular long-grain rice, rinsed
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 3–3½ cups chicken stock (as needed)
  • 500 g chicken (thighs preferred), cut bite-size
  • 1 chorizo (or longganisa), sliced (optional)
  • 1 onion + 4 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 thumb ginger, minced (optional)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder (or fresh grated)
  • 1 Tbsp annatto (achuete) oil (optional, for color)
  • 1 carrot (diced), ½ cup green peas, 1 bell pepper (strips)
  • 2–3 eggs, hard-boiled and sliced (for topping)
  • Banana leaves (for lining; optional but authentic)
  • Salt & pepper, oil

Steps

  1. Sauté aromatics: In a wide pan, heat oil/annatto oil. Sauté onion, garlic (and ginger if using).
  2. Brown meats: Add chicken (and chorizo). Season with salt/pepper.
  3. Toast the rice: Add both rices; stir 1–2 mins until glossy.
  4. Add liquids & color: Pour in coconut milk and 3 cups stock. Stir in turmeric.
  5. Simmer: Cover and cook on low, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Add more stock if drying too fast.
  6. Add veggies: When rice is nearly tender, fold in carrot, peas, and half the bell pepper. Adjust seasoning.
  7. Finish (banana leaf method): Line a second pan or the same pan with banana leaves, spoon bringhe on top, cover, and steam 5–10 mins for aroma and a light crust.
  8. Garnish: Top with egg slices and remaining bell pepper. Rest 5 mins before slicing/serving.

Pro Tips

  • Right texture: Slightly sticky but not mushy; mix malagkit with regular rice.
  • Protein swap: Shrimp, mussels, or tofu also work—add near the end so they don’t overcook.
